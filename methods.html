Methods

At WayLight Atlantic, the ethos is that technology should serve people, not overwhelm them.

The work focuses on clarity, simplicity, and sustainability: building systems and websites that remain understandable years from now, not just on launch day.

WayLight methods are inspired by traditional disciplines, including monastic ways of working: identify what is truly needed, preserve what is useful, and remove what creates unnecessary burden.

Most organisations can apply these methods independently, and this website is designed to explain how. WayLight provides delivery support for teams that want the same outcomes with dedicated time, energy, and competitive pricing. Demonstration builds are available on the Workbench.

This is done using conventional technology that people can realistically use and maintain. Where newer tools add clear value, WayLight can guide clients through practical adoption, including appropriate use of Artificial Intelligence.

01-05

End-to-end delivery process

The method is structured in five clear stages so decisions stay visible and delivery stays calm.

  • 1. Discovery

    Understand the organisation before proposing solutions.

    Includes: conversation with stakeholders, review of current systems or website, and identification of real problems.

    Outputs: simple project brief and priority list.

  • 2. Clarification

    Create a clear digital structure before design decisions.

    Includes: simplifying information, removing duplication, and identifying essential pages or processes.

    Outputs: site structure, workflow map, and recommendations.

  • 3. Design and Build

    Delivery emphasises simplicity over complexity.

    Approach: lightweight websites, fast loading pages, minimal dependencies, accessibility, and clear navigation.

    Typical stack: static sites, modern HTML/CSS, minimal JavaScript, and reliable hosting.

  • 4. Documentation

    Every project includes plain documentation so organisations are not dependent on one person forever.

    Included: simple instructions, structured files, and maintainable systems.

  • 5. Stewardship (Optional)

    For organisations that want ongoing support after launch.

    Examples: website updates, digital hygiene checks, backups, security monitoring, and small improvements.

    This is offered as a low-cost monthly stewardship model (typically £20-£40 per month, depending on scope). See Services & Pricing for current package details.

Technology Choices

Choosing reliable over fragile

WayLight favours approaches that are easier to maintain, faster to run, and less likely to break without warning.

Approach Why
Static websites Fast, secure, and low-cost to host.
Minimal plugins Reduces breakage and update overhead.
Clear file structure Makes long-term maintenance easier for teams.
Open standards Helps avoid vendor lock-in and preserves portability.

In practice, this means avoiding heavily plugin-driven builds that become difficult to maintain.
