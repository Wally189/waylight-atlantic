Digital Hygiene

Small rules that stop big chaos.

Digital hygiene is the boring, dependable stuff: how you name things, where you save them, who can access them, and what happens when someone is off sick.

Focus

  • Folders and naming
  • Access and permissions
  • Backups and handovers

The six essentials

  1. 01

    Inbox rules

    Inbox is not storage. It’s a doorway. Process, park, or bin.

  2. 02

    Folder spine

    One small structure that stays stable. No endless “misc.”

  3. 03

    Naming convention

    Dates, versions, owners—so searching works and duplicates stop.

  4. 04

    Access hygiene

    Right people, right level. Remove stragglers. Reduce single points of failure.

  5. 05

    Backups

    Test restores, not just backups. Label owners and review dates.

  6. 06

    Runbook

    A one-page “how to run it” so cover is easy when someone is out.

What happens when it’s ignored?

Lost hours, lost files, confused handovers, and brittle dependency on one person.

Signals you need a tidy

  • “It’s in my inbox somewhere.”
  • Different versions emailed around.
  • People leave, access stays.
  • Nobody knows where the “current” file is.
  • No backups, or backups never tested.
